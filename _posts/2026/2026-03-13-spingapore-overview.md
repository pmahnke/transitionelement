In March 2026 we spent little over a week visiting Singapore.  It was really Owen's holiday that we invited ourselves along to.

While we really liked visiting, I think eight days was too much.  Four would have been perfect, perhaps on a stopover heading somewhere else in the region.

### Apps and money

We did get a bit of cash before we left. However, we found that nearly everywhere took and prefered cards.  The notable exception was the Hawker Centres. Many stalls only took cash.  We loved and had a lot of meals at these centres, so we found it useful to have some.

### Mobile data

I ended up getting Singtel virtual SIMs via [Pelago](https://www.pelago.com/en-GB/activity/p8pl4-singtel-prepaid-sim-card-singapore/) before we left.  They were 5G and 500GB for £2.80 and worked perfectly.  I am sure you could get something similar even at the airport.

We ended up using Pelago to get tickets to the Gardens by the Bay as well.

### Getting around

You can use your phone or card on the tubes and buses, no need to get a special card.  We ended up mostly using buses with the help of Google.  But the tubes and buses are all easy to use and clean.

### Hotels

We picked a hotel, [The White House](https://www.booking.com/hotel/sg/hovoh-homes-bugis-beach.en-gb.html), in Bugis, a good central location. Close to the metro.  It was spacious and had a kitchen. We didn't spend a lot of time there, so didn't want to spend a lot for something fancier.

### Power

In Singapore, power plugs and sockets are type G. This is the same as the UK. The standard voltage is 230 V at a frequency of 50 Hz. We didn't have to do anything special as we live in London.

### Language

There are many languages spoken in Singapore, Malay, Chinese, Hindi, but English is the official language and it is spoken everywhere.

### Food

One of the reasons to go is the high quality food from all over Asia and Europe. Every meal we had was top class.  We ate a lot of Chinese noodles and dumplings as well as some Indian, Turkish, and Japanese food.  The Hawker Centres are really easy, inexpensive, and often some of the best food we had, I cannot recommend them enough.

### Costs

Singapore is not cheap compared to nearby countries.  I would say it approaches European prices.  However, bus trips were cheap, as was food in Hawker Centres.


### Visas

We didn't need a visa to visit, but we did fill in an [arrival card](https://eservices.ica.gov.sg/sgarrivalcard/) before we flew. It gets associated with your passport and you basically scan your passport and walk out the building.  Easiest immigration and customes ever.
