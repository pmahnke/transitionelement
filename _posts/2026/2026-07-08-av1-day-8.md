We started by going down.  Even that was tough.  We did a long road walk, and then started up  the base of the San Sebastiano and Tamer massifs, but a couple of kilometers in, I knew I had to stop.

Angela wasn't happy, but knew I needed to stop.  We made our way back to the very empty road.  I spoke to an Italian day hiker who said we should just walk down to Agordo, some 14 kilometers away, and try hitchhiking.

Angela was trying to cancel our rifugio, and I saw our first van, so I stuck my thumb out.  The driver stopped and said, 'I can give you a ride, but I am a taxi, not a free car'.  It turned out it was 80 euros to Belluno.  We jumped in.

On the way, we canceled our next three stops and booked a lovely place in Belunno for a night and two nights in Venice.

We walked around Belluno and bought a few things for Angela's next trip to Pakistan.  It is a lovely, but quiet town.

Horrible to come off the trail, a great recovery finding the taxi.
