### 29 June to 10 July 2026

#### Lago di Braies to Passo Duran. 60 miles (97 kilometers) - 8 days

### Booking refugios

This is the by far the biggest pain.  They all have different days booking is opened and they spots go fast.  We made a spreadsheet of everything we wanted and had a lot of late nights.

My one thing I would say is, there are a lot of huts that are slightly off the main trail. Since you are walking hours a day, another twenty minutes off the trail is nothing.  So if you can't get the exact hut you want, look a bit off the main trail, and you will likely find things.

However, you probably still need to book these promptly when they open, which might be ten or more months ahead of your hike.

### Getting there

The most obvious way is to fly into Venice.  From the airport there is the Cortina Express.  You should [book this](https://www.cortinaexpress.it/en/) ahead of time.

From Cortina, you take a different bus to Dobbiaco.  This is at a slightly different location in Cortina. I think you need to book these in July and August, you can get tickets at the Tourist Information office, right where the Cortina Express stops.  We travelled on the 30th June, so there were no issues.

Once in Dobbiaco, you just move about ten feet and take a final bus to Lago di Braies.

We stopped in Cortina for a night, but we are retired and have lots of time.  You could do this in a day if you needed.

### The trail

The trail is very well marked.  If not with AV1 triangles, with actual route numbers and usually point to refugios or passes.  Definately use a map or device, but we just wrote down the path numbers and refugios we would pass and turned at the signs.

The trail was generally in very good condition.  Only over scree and boulder fields was it sometimes hard to follow, but usually there were cairns and ways to find the correct trail.

### Refugios

If you haven't done hut to hut hiking, it is pretty simple.  You usually are in a room of bunk beds.  The refugio supplies a pillow and blanket.  You need to bring a sleeping bag liner to cover your pillow and get in to sleep.  We bring light silk liners.  

There was wifi or signal at nearly every refugio.  We didn't use our Garmin InReach once. They also have some way to charge devices.  I only used my battery pack one time.

You get dinner and breakfast at each refugio.  They also offer pack lunches that you can order at dinner.  However, we either ate some tortillas with peanut butter and jam, or stopped at refugios that were serving lunch on the walk.

I should also say, they all have excellent bars with beer, wine, coffees, teas, soft drinks, snacks, and cakes that you can get at any time.

### Money

Everything is in Euros.  All but one refugio took card, so I would say, bring some cash.  But it was only a single place.
